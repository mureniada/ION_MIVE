# Epistemology

## Foundational statement

**Intelligence does not equal truth.**

A strong model can still:
- hallucinate;
- overgeneralize;
- omit evidence;
- confuse interpretation with fact;
- express unjustified confidence.

Truth is therefore not treated as a direct model output.

## Operational path toward validated knowledge

Independent intelligence
→ structured reports
→ comparison
→ evidence inspection
→ uncertainty exposure
→ operator validation
→ validated knowledge

## Core distinctions

### Information
Source material stored in the corpus.

### Retrieval
Selection of potentially relevant source fragments. Retrieval does not reason and does not validate.

### Intelligence
A model-generated interpretation of a fixed Context Pack.

### Evidence
Traceable source fragments that support or challenge claims.

### Validation
A process that tests structure, traceability, agreement, contradiction, and uncertainty.

### Knowledge
A conclusion that has passed the required validation boundary. The first local product does not automatically publish knowledge.

## Epistemic rules

1. Evidence before confidence.
2. Every important claim must be traceable to one or more corpus documents.
3. Missing evidence must remain visible.
4. Disagreement is information, not failure.
5. Unique findings must remain attributable to the originating engine.
6. Model confidence must never be presented as proof.
7. The final answer must preserve uncertainty rather than smoothing it away.
8. Independent model paths must remain independent until MIVE comparison.
9. Reproducibility requires fixed inputs, recorded model identifiers, and recorded evidence.
10. Operator authority remains above automated comparison.
